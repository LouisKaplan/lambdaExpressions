Lambda Expressions - Emulating Functional Programming

Newly supported in Java8, Lambda Expressions make functional programming available for a wide range of uses. They give developers a quick and succinct way to essentially pass a behavior - as opposed to passing an object WITH a behavior.

In this demo, we'll go over the basic syntax of Lambda Expressions, talk about why they make use of interfaces, and look at some examples. By the end, you should have the foundation necessary to begin assigning code itself to variables, rather than just the values of the corresponding blocks of code.

At first glance, Lambda Expressions simply look like a shorthand way of writing the same code, but we'll take a look at an example of them in use, and hopefully demonstrate their value both a timesaver and as an option for added flexibility.
